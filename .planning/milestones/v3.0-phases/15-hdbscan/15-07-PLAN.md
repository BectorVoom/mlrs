<critical_typestate_nuance>
DO NOT blindly transcribe the verifier's `missing:` proposal ‚Äî it copied
DBSCAN's `&mut self` signature, which will NOT compile for `Hdbscan`. The two
estimators differ at the typestate level:

- **DBSCAN**: `fit(&mut self, ‚Ä¶) -> Result<&mut Self, ‚Ä¶>` ‚Äî `self` survives the
  fit, so `fit_predict(&mut self, ‚Ä¶)` can call `self.fit(‚Ä¶)?` then `self.labels(‚Ä¶)`.
- **Hdbscan**: `Fit::fit` **CONSUMES `self`** and returns a NEW
  `Hdbscan<F, Fitted>` (hdbscan.rs:463-469, and the module doc at lines 24/120).
  A `&mut self` wrapper that fits and then reads `labels_` cannot compile ‚Äî
  after `Fit::fit(self, ‚Ä¶)` the original estimator is moved away.

The executor MUST read `hdbscan.rs` to confirm the exact mechanics (signature of
`Fit::fit`, where `labels()` lives, its return type) and derive the correct
signature FROM the code, not from the verifier's proposal.
</critical_typestate_nuance>

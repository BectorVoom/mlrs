On the deterministic tier (`bootstrap=false, max_features=All`) every tree in the mlrs forest is therefore **bit-identical** to every other tree (zero stochastic divergence across trees) — which means "sum-raw-decrease-then-normalize-once" (mlrs's chosen formula) and "normalize-per-tree-then-average" (sklearn's formula) are provably equal: if every tree's total raw-decrease `S_t` is identical (which it must be if the trees are identical), `(1/T)Σ_t(raw_t,f/S) = (Σ_t raw_t,f)/(T·S)`, and after each side's own separate normalization to sum 1, both reduce to the identical value.
